Introduction

Chickens display a variety of interesting behaviors. Understanding these can help in caring for them and observing their social interactions.

Common Chicken Behaviors

  • Pecking: Used to search for food or establish dominance.
  • Dust Bathing: Chickens clean themselves by rolling in dust.
  • Brooding: Hens sit on eggs to incubate them.
  • Foraging: Searching for seeds, insects, and plants.
  • Roosting: Perching on elevated spots to sleep.

Behavior Table

Behavior Description Purpose
Pecking Order Hierarchy established by pecking Social structure
Clucking Vocal communication Alert, call chicks, express contentment
Preening Cleaning and arranging feathers Feather maintenance
Scratching Using feet to uncover food Foraging

Behavioral Sequence

  1. Wake up and leave the roost.
  2. Forage for food.
  3. Take a dust bath.
  4. Interact with flock members.
  5. Return to roost at dusk.
