Introduction

Proper nutrition is essential for chickens to maintain good health, produce quality eggs, and grow efficiently. Different diets offer unique benefits for chickens at various life stages.

Common Chicken Diets & Their Health Benefits

Starter Feed (Chicks)

  • High protein (18-20%) for rapid growth
  • Balanced vitamins and minerals for bone development
  • Crumbled texture for easy consumption

Grower Feed (Pullets)

  • Moderate protein (16-18%) for steady growth
  • Supports immune system development
  • Prepares chickens for laying phase

Layer Feed (Laying Hens)

  • Added calcium for strong eggshells
  • Balanced protein (16%) for egg production
  • Essential vitamins for reproductive health

Scratch Grains

  • Provides energy and encourages natural foraging
  • Should be fed in moderation

Supplemental Greens & Vegetables

  • Rich in vitamins and antioxidants
  • Improves digestion and overall health

Tips for a Balanced Chicken Diet

  1. Provide clean, fresh water at all times.
  2. Adjust feed type as chickens grow.
  3. Offer grit to aid digestion, especially with whole grains.
  4. Limit treats to avoid nutritional imbalance.
  5. Consult a veterinarian for specific dietary needs.
