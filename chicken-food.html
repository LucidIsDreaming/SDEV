Starter Feed

Starter feed is designed for baby chicks. It is high in protein (around 20-24%) to support rapid growth and development.

  • Fine texture for easy eating
  • Fed from hatch to 6 weeks

Grower Feed

Grower feed is given to adolescent chickens, usually from 6 to 20 weeks old. It contains less protein than starter feed (16-18%) and helps chickens grow at a healthy rate.

  • Supports steady growth
  • Prepares pullets for laying

Layer Feed

Layer feed is formulated for hens that are laying eggs. It contains extra calcium to support eggshell production and has balanced nutrients for adult chickens.

  • Fed from 20 weeks onward
  • Higher calcium content

Scratch Grains

Scratch grains are a mix of cracked corn and other grains. They are not a complete feed but are given as a treat to encourage natural foraging behavior.

  • Should be fed in moderation
  • Not nutritionally complete

Treats & Supplements

Chickens enjoy treats like fruits, vegetables, and mealworms. Supplements such as oyster shell and grit help with digestion and eggshell quality.

  • Offer treats sparingly
  • Provide grit for digestion

Quick Facts

  1. Starter feed is for chicks.
  2. Grower feed is for young chickens.
  3. Layer feed is for egg-laying hens.
  4. Scratch grains are treats, not main food.
  5. Supplements support health and digestion.
